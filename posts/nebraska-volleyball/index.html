Why has Nebraska Volleyball struggled to secure an NCAA Tournament title despite dominating the regular season?

nebraska
volleyball

Author

Marlee Pinnt

Published

April 10, 2026

Nebraska remains one of the strongest programs in the country, consistently entering the postseason with top rankings, dominant records, and high expectations. However, despite their dominance in the regular season, the Huskers have repeatedly fallen short of a national title over the past few seasons, often coming heartbreakingly close.

This past 2025 season, the Huskers finished the season at 33-1. They opened the NCAA Tournament with a three-set sweep over Kansas State and advanced through the Sweet 16 by beating No. 16 Kansas, again in three straight sets. However, the Huskers’ run ended in the Elite 8 after a hard-fought, five-set battle against Texas A&M, who went on to win the title. Despite the Huskers’ tough end to the season, Nebraska remains the standard that many programs and fans look to.

Is Nebraska an outlier in finishing short of its goal? Compared to other top seeds, the answer is no. Several teams saw their rankings sihft between the start of the tournament and the final standings. In the final top 10, Nebraska and Texas saw the largest drops: Nebraska fell from No. 1 to No. 3, while Texas dropped from No. 3 to No. 6. Other teams also used the tournament to improve their rankings. Texas A&M and Wisconsin saw the most improvement, both jumping five spots: Texas A&M from No. 6 to No. 1, and Wisconsin from No. 10 to No.5.

Since Nebraska isn’t particularly an outlier in terms of shifting rankings, what could be other reasons? Could it be that the team is underperforming statistically once entering the tournament? Nebraska shows the greatest difference in the average errors, digs, and assist categories from the regular to the postseason.

From the table, we can see Nebraska’s stats as a team remain mostly consistent from the regular season into the postseason. So the issue may be that their opponents are overperforming at the right time in categories like hitting percentage, passing, serve receive, and error ratio.

Overall, Wisconsin shows the greatest postseason improvements statistically. Wisconsin and Pitt improved their hitting percentage the most, Wisconsin and Texas A&M improved their average kills by the largest margin, and Pitt improved their error ratio the most.

Ultimately, Nebraska fans shouldn’t feel discouraged. For the most part, the Huskers remains consistent in their level of play in the final rounds. The data shows they are consistently losing to the highest level of competition. In the past ten seasons, the Huskers have appeared in five championship matches, claiming the title twice: in 2015 against Texas and in 2017 against Florida. Even when Nebraska was eliminated early, they tend to lose to the best; since 2021, they have been knocked out by the eventual National Champion four times, including each of the last three seasons.
